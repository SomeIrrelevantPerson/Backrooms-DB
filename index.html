DESCRIPTION

Level 0 is the most commonly reported initial entry point into the Backrooms. It manifests as an enormous, seemingly infinite expanse of empty office space — characterized by aged, mono-yellow wallpaper, stained damp carpet, and the persistent hum of fluorescent lights that stretch endlessly overhead.

No windows. No doors. No visible ceiling beyond the light fixtures. The layout follows no architectural logic: corridors fold back on themselves, rooms appear and disappear, and long-term navigation by memory is considered unreliable and potentially dangerous.

The smell has been consistently described across hundreds of accounts as wet carpet and old adhesive. The fluorescent lights flicker at irregular intervals. Some wanderers have reported brief, complete blackouts lasting seconds to minutes. What happens during these blackouts is largely undocumented.

ENVIRONMENT

Temperature is reported consistently between 18–22°C (64–72°F). Humidity is high, typically above 65%, which accounts for the dampness of surfaces despite no apparent water source. The carpet — a mottled, institutional yellow-beige — shows signs of old staining in many areas but appears structurally intact.

Sound does not carry normally. Footsteps are muffled. Voices feel absorbed by the walls. Several accounts describe hearing distant footsteps or sounds that do not belong to the wanderer — though no source has ever been confirmed on Level 0.

KNOWN ENTITIES
SMILERS
● HOSTILE · CONFIRMED PRESENCE
Detected primarily during light failures. Distinguished by a pair of luminescent eyes visible in darkness. Do not approach. Do not acknowledge. Maintain calm movement toward light.
FACELINGS
● PASSIVE · CONFIRMED PRESENCE
Humanoid figures lacking facial features. Generally non-aggressive. Avoid sustained eye contact and sudden movement. They have not been reported to initiate contact.
WINDOWS
● HOSTILE · LETHAL ON CONTACT
Manifest as ordinary-looking windows set into the walls of Level 0 — a level that has no windows. Often display an exterior view: daylight, streets, sky, rooms beyond. A silhouette is frequently observed on the other side, still or slowly moving. Do not approach. Wanderers who have come within arm's reach have been pulled through without warning. No account of recovery exists.

Entity encounters on Level 0 increase significantly during light failures. If lights begin to flicker, move immediately toward the brightest accessible area. Do not remain stationary in dimly lit corridors. Additionally: if you see a window — any window — do not approach it. Level 0 has no windows. Whatever you are looking at is not one.

SURVIVAL GUIDELINES
  • Do not run. Rapid movement draws attention and rapidly depletes caloric reserves in an environment with no confirmed food sources.
  • Move toward sound. Distant hum variations may indicate structural differences — potential exit points or transitions to other levels.
  • Mark your path if possible. The environment is disorienting by nature; physical markers help prevent circular wandering.
  • Maintain psychological stability. Panic has been documented as the primary cause of poor decision-making and fatal outcomes on Level 0.
  • Exit method: Noclipping through a structurally weakened wall section — typically identified by a subtle warping or visual distortion — is the most documented method of progression.
  • Do not separate from companions. Solo wanderers have a significantly lower documented return rate.
EXIT POINTS

The primary documented exit from Level 0 leads to Level 1 (The Parking Zone). Transition occurs via noclipping through certain wall sections — identifiable by a faint visual shimmer or tactile softness in the surface material. The exact conditions that make a wall passable are not fully understood.

Some accounts mention spontaneous transitions occurring during light failures, but given the hazard conditions during such events, this method is not recommended.

DESCRIPTION

Level 1 presents a dramatic shift from Level 0. Where the Lobby is intimate and office-like, the Parking Zone is vast and industrial — an endless concrete expanse of shipping crates, metal shelving, loading bays, and rusted machinery. The ceiling is lost to darkness even during periods of light. The scale is deeply disorienting.

Lighting here is inconsistent and unreliable. Functional fluorescent strips illuminate some areas; large sections remain in permanent near-darkness. The source of any light in the dark zones is unknown. Several wanderers have noted that light conditions appear to shift between visits to the same physical location — as though the level is not static.

Unlike Level 0, Level 1 contains material resources: water is reliably found in pipes throughout the level, and sealed food containers have been recovered from shelving units. This makes Level 1 a critical survival zone for wanderers who establish themselves within it — though the entity presence significantly complicates long-term habitation.

ENVIRONMENT

The floor is rough concrete, frequently cracked and uneven. Wanderers have reported deep gouges and drag marks in the flooring that pre-date any known human presence. Temperatures range from 12–18°C (54–64°F) — noticeably cooler than Level 0. The air carries a metallic, industrial smell overlaid with rust and machine oil.

Sound propagates strangely. In open areas, footsteps echo with unusual persistence. In corridors between shelving units, sound is deadened. Machinery can be heard operating somewhere in the distance — rhythmic, mechanical sounds that never seem to get closer regardless of direction traveled.

Several survivors describe discovering what appear to be makeshift campsites — bedding, rationed supplies, notes — apparently left by previous wanderers. The condition of these sites varies wildly. Some appear recently abandoned; others have been undisturbed for what may be years.

CLUMPS

Large, slow-moving masses of unclear composition. Accounts conflict on threat level. Some report them as passive; others describe aggressive behavior following prolonged proximity. Maintain distance.

Hound activity increases dramatically in the dark sectors of Level 1. Current recommendation is to avoid any area where ambient light drops below functional visibility. If caught in darkness — stop moving, control breathing, and wait.

SURVIVAL GUIDELINES

  • Secure water before exploring further. Pipe locations near the entry zone from Level 0 are the most reliably documented.
  • Search abandoned campsites carefully. Prior occupants' notes have navigated wanderers to exits; this information is invaluable.
  • Do not move through dark sectors under any circumstances if Hound activity has been detected in the vicinity.
  • The machinery sounds serve as a rough compass. Wanderers who followed the sound consistently have reported reaching structural anomalies that led to level transitions.
  • If establishing a camp, choose an elevated position with at least one clear sightline. Ground-level positions have a worse documented survival rate.

DESCRIPTION

Level 2 is a vast network of industrial maintenance tunnels — low ceilings, exposed pipework running in all directions, and near-total darkness broken only by the faint glow of heat venting from the pipes themselves. The architecture suggests a massive utility infrastructure, but one that serves no discernible purpose and connects to nothing outside itself.

The pipes are hot. Surface temperatures on the larger diameter conduits reach 60–80°C (140–176°F), creating an ambient heat that makes prolonged traversal physically taxing. The air is thick with steam and condensation. Visibility without a light source is effectively zero — and light sources are consumed faster here than on other levels, as though the environment resists illumination.

Unlike Levels 0 and 1, Level 2 is audibly alive. The pipes groan, clank, and hiss continuously. Some wanderers report rhythmic thumping deep within the walls — consistent, almost mechanical, but never attributable to a known source. This constant noise makes it nearly impossible to detect entity movement by sound alone, which dramatically increases encounter risk.

IMG_0078 · LEVEL 2 · TUNNEL NETWORK · SECTOR UNKNOWN
ENVIRONMENT

The tunnels range from just over a meter (3.3 ft) in height to approximately four meters (13 ft) — though most documented passages fall in the 1.8–2.5m (6–8 ft) range. Wider junctions exist where multiple pipe conduits converge, creating open chambers of ten meters (33 ft) or more. These junction points are simultaneously the most navigable spaces and the most dangerous, given increased entity activity.

The floor is slick — a combination of condensation runoff and an unidentified dark liquid that seeps from the lower pipe joints. Falls are common. The footing requires constant attention. Several accounts note that the liquid is warm to the touch and leaves a faint metallic residue, but no chemical analysis has been possible.

Heat management is a critical survival factor. Core body temperature elevation has been reported in wanderers who remain without rest for over two hours. The heat is not uniform — some corridors are noticeably cooler, and these have been identified as priority resting areas.

Far more numerous here than Level 1. The pipe network appears to be their native habitat. Exploit the noise of the pipes — you cannot hear them coming. Keep moving. Never stop in a low-clearance section.

● HOSTILE · CONFIRMED
Large, dark-winged entities drawn to heat and light. Attacks are disorienting rather than direct — they extinguish light sources. Do not use open flame. LED sources are less likely to attract them but not immune.
WRETCHES
● HOSTILE · JUNCTION ZONES
Reported exclusively at pipe junction chambers. Humanoid but elongated, moving along the pipes rather than the floor. Avoid junction chambers during perceived high-activity periods. Listen before entering.
SMILERS
● HOSTILE · DARK ZONES
Present in the darkest tunnel sections. Same behavior as Level 0. The near-total darkness of Level 2 makes avoiding them far more difficult. Maintaining any light source is not optional here.

Level 2 has the highest confirmed entity density of any documented level in this archive. Wanderers who stop moving for extended periods in unlit areas have not been heard from again. This is not a level to explore — it is a level to traverse as quickly as possible.

Found in small alcoves between pipe clusters. More reliably located here than Level 1. Its heat-regulation properties appear more pronounced in this environment — a high priority find.

SURVIVAL GUIDELINES
  • Protect your light source above all else. Entering darkness on Level 2 is documented as near-fatal in the majority of accounts.
  • Never stop moving in narrow tunnels. Crawlers exploit the pipe noise as cover — static targets are significantly easier to locate than moving ones.
  • Mark pipe junctions before entering. They look identical in low light and disorientation is rapid and severe.
  • Move toward cooler air. Cool zones indicate either a transition point or a safer resting area. Follow temperature drops deliberately.
  • Do not attempt to climb the pipes. Deathmoths roost above the primary bundles and disturbance triggers aggressive behavior.
  • If light fails: crouch, remain still, control breathing. Wait a minimum of five minutes before moving. Noise discipline is your only defense.

DESCRIPTION

Level 3 is an enormous, sprawling electrical substation of impossible scale — a labyrinth of humming transformer banks, fuse boxes, switchgear panels, and thick cable bundles running floor to ceiling. Everything here is charged. The air itself carries a persistent static that raises hair on exposed skin and produces a constant low-frequency vibration in the chest cavity. Wanderers have reported tasting metal within minutes of arrival.

Lighting is paradoxically plentiful — harsh, blue-white fluorescent strips illuminate most areas in stark detail. This is, in most respects, the last advantage Level 3 offers. The brightness does not represent safety. Entity density here rivals Level 2, and the entities native to this level are significantly more dangerous than those encountered in previous levels.

The electrical infrastructure is active and lethally so. Exposed wiring, open panel boxes, and crackling junction points are everywhere. Multiple confirmed fatalities are attributed directly to electrical contact rather than entity encounters. Touch nothing metal that is not specifically confirmed inert. The floor carries low-level current in many sections — rubber or thick-soled footwear is not optional.

IMG_0103 · LEVEL 3 · SUBSTATION GRID · ZONE UNKNOWN
ENVIRONMENT

The space is divided into corridors between enormous transformer banks and switchgear arrays. Many corridors are less than a meter (3.3 ft) wide, forcing wanderers into close proximity with live electrical equipment on both sides. The transformer units radiate significant heat in addition to the low hum of operating current — making this level both electrically and thermally hazardous.

Cable bundles descend from the ceiling in dense clusters, some pooling on the floor. These are particularly dangerous: wanderers have reported cables that appear inert but carry lethal current, indistinguishable from dead lines without testing equipment. Assume all cables are live.

Sections of Level 3 experience periodic full power surges — brief but intense flashes of light across all panels, accompanied by a deep electrical crack. During surges, arc flash events can occur unpredictably. Wanderers in the vicinity of open junction boxes during a surge have a very poor documented survival rate. When you feel the static intensify, find cover immediately.

KNOWN ENTITIES
BURSTERS
● HOSTILE · APEX THREAT
Native to Level 3. Tall, thin, move in near silence. Their eyes emit a faint blue-white light — the only warning of approach. They appear to feed on or interact with the electrical infrastructure. Do not approach any area showing unusual panel activity.
DEATHMOTHS
● HOSTILE · HIGH DENSITY
More numerous here than Level 2, drawn to the abundant electrical light. Paradoxically, extinguishing a personal light source is more dangerous here than leaving it on — the ambient light is sufficient to keep them circling rather than attacking.
CLUMPS
● THREAT VARIABLE · SURGE-LINKED
Appear during and shortly after power surges, congregating near active junction boxes and open panels. Slow-moving but drawn toward light and heat sources. Behavior shifts from passive to aggressive without clear provocation. Clear the area when a surge ends — do not linger to observe them.
HOUNDS
● HOSTILE · MIGRATED FROM L1
Smaller population than Level 1 but present. Appear disoriented by the electrical environment — erratic movement patterns, less effective hunting behavior. Still dangerous. The electrical hum masks their approach sounds.

Bursters are the highest-threat entity documented in this archive to date. They have been observed interacting with the electrical panels in ways that trigger localized surges. If panels near you begin activating without apparent cause, vacate the area immediately at a controlled pace. Do not run — Bursters respond strongly to rapid movement.

AVAILABLE RESOURCES
WATER TANKS
● UNCOMMON
Industrial cooling water tanks attached to transformer units. Water quality is generally poor but survivable. Do not attempt to access tanks near active electrical components — water and live current in this environment is fatal.
RUBBER GLOVES
● UNCOMMON · CRITICAL
Electrical insulation gloves found in safety lockers along the walls. Rated to 10,000V in documented finds. Finding a pair before exploring further is strongly advised — they dramatically expand what can be safely interacted with.
SAFETY LOCKERS
● UNCOMMON
Wall-mounted lockers intended for electrical safety equipment. Contents vary: rubber gloves, insulated boots, face shields, voltage testers. Some have been emptied by prior wanderers. Worth checking every one encountered.
ALMOND WATER
● RARE
Rarely found in locked cabinets accessible via pried-open doors. Presence here is unexplained — no one has documented placing it. Its static-dampening effect (reduced skin charge sensation) has been noted by multiple accounts.
SURVIVAL GUIDELINES
  • Do not touch any metal surface without rubber insulation. This is not precautionary — it is the difference between survival and death within the first hour.
  • When static intensifies noticeably, stop moving and find non-conductive cover. A power surge is imminent. Wait until the hum returns to baseline before continuing.
  • Move through the level during low-activity periods. Burster sightings cluster around surge events — between surges is the safest window.
  • Do not look directly at arc flash events. The light intensity causes temporary blindness, and momentary blindness on Level 3 is extremely dangerous.
  • Keep moving between panel banks. Open floor space is safer than narrow cable corridors — prioritize wider paths even if they take longer.
  • Locate safety lockers as your first objective. Rubber insulation gear obtained within the first thirty minutes dramatically increases survival probability.

DESCRIPTION

Level 4 represents a disquieting return to the familiar — an endless sprawl of abandoned office space, far more developed than Level 0. Where Level 0 was empty and featureless, Level 4 is furnished: rows of desks, filing cabinets, cubicle partitions, break rooms, meeting rooms, server racks, and corridors lined with closed office doors. Every detail suggests a building that was once occupied and simply… wasn't anymore.

Nothing is overtly wrong with Level 4. That is part of what makes it unsettling. The fluorescent lights work. The carpet, though dusty, is intact. Clocks hang on walls — none of them running, but none of them broken either, needles stopped at times that differ from room to room. Coffee mugs sit on desks. Filing cabinets hold papers in languages wanderers have been unable to identify. The computers, where found, display only a blank cursor on a black screen.

Despite its relative comfort compared to Levels 2 and 3, Level 4 is not safe. Its entity population is sparse but specifically dangerous in enclosed spaces, and the psychological weight of the level is well documented — long-term habitation produces a creeping disorientation that wanderers describe as forgetting what they were doing here, or why they left wherever they came from.

IMG_0231 · LEVEL 4 · ADMINISTRATIVE WING · SECTION C
ENVIRONMENT

The office infrastructure is extensive. Functioning water coolers have been found — still cold, still dispensing, with no apparent power connection. Vending machines exist in break rooms, some operational, dispensing products whose branding is unfamiliar and whose contents, while apparently safe, have an indeterminate taste described as "almost right." Bathrooms are present and functional, though mirrors in these spaces have been reported to behave anomalously — not visually, but in a subtler way wanderers struggle to articulate.

Temperature is mild and consistent, around 20°C (68°F). The air is stale but breathable. Sound is muffled by the carpet and cubicle partitions, creating pockets of near-complete silence. In these silence zones, some wanderers report hearing typing from adjacent rooms — rhythmic, consistent, stopping immediately if approached. Investigation has never identified a source.

Papers and documents are found throughout, but their content is universally either in unrecognizable scripts or in English that is grammatically correct but semantically incoherent — professional-sounding text that conveys no meaning on inspection. Wanderers are advised not to spend significant time attempting to parse these documents.

KNOWN ENTITIES
HOUNDS
● HOSTILE · SPARSE
Present in low numbers compared to Level 1. Hunt by sound — the carpeted floors and soft acoustics of Level 4 actually work in your favor. Move quietly, avoid running, and they rarely close in. Still lethal if cornered.
DULLERS
● THREAT UNCLEAR · OCCASIONAL
Encountered drifting slowly through corridors or standing motionless in offices. Behavior remains inconclusive — no confirmed attacks on Level 4, but proximity reports are unsettling. Give them a wide berth and do not linger near them.

Entity presence on Level 4 is low but not absent. Hounds are the primary active threat — silence discipline is your best defense. Dullers are largely passive but their behavior is not fully understood. Do not become complacent in what appears to be a safe environment.

AVAILABLE RESOURCES

● COMMON · RELIABLE
Functional and cold. The most reliable water source of any documented level. Source of water is unknown. Consumption has not produced illness in any documented account.
VENDING MACHINES

Operational. Products are unfamiliar branded items — chips, crackers, bars — that are calorie-dense and edible, though taste descriptions are consistently strange. A significant caloric resource for wanderers reaching this level.

● UNCOMMON
Wall-mounted in corridors and break rooms. Fully stocked in most finds — bandages, antiseptic, basic analgesics. The best medical resource documented in the archive to date.
ALMOND WATER
● UNCOMMON
Found in desk drawers and break room cabinets with unusual regularity compared to other levels. Its psychological stabilizing effect is particularly valuable here given the level's documented cognitive effects on long-term occupants.
SURVIVAL GUIDELINES
  • Do not disturb the Facelings. They are present in significant numbers and their passive behavior appears conditional — accounts of provoked Faceling groups are not recoverable accounts.
  • Establish a companion verification protocol before entering any closed room together. Skin-Stealers exploit familiarity — the longer you have been with someone, the more material they have to work with.
  • Do not try to read the documents. The time investment yields nothing and several accounts report a lingering confusion following extended reading attempts.
  • Use the vending machines and water coolers. Level 4 is the best resupply point in the archive. Prioritize caloric intake before continuing descent.
  • Do not sleep in rooms with Facelings present. Relocate to an empty office, barricade the door, and take watch rotations if traveling with others.
  • If you hear typing from a room you know to be empty — do not open the door.

DESCRIPTION

Level 5 presents as an infinite hotel of indeterminate era — long carpeted corridors lined with numbered doors, ice machine alcoves, stairwells, lobbies, function rooms, and the occasional indoor pool glimpsed through porthole windows. The aesthetic is neither modern nor dated but occupies an uncomfortable middle space, like a building remembered rather than built. The carpet pattern is consistent throughout: a deep burgundy and gold diamond weave that has been described in every account with slight variations — as though the pattern subtly differs depending on who is looking at it.

The hotel is not empty. This is the critical distinction between Level 5 and Level 4. Level 4 felt abandoned. Level 5 feels recently vacated. Ice machines hum. Televisions in rooms left ajar display static or, in some accounts, programming in unknown languages. Room service trays sit in corridors with food in various states — some fresh, some long cold, none explicable. The beds in accessible rooms are made. The towels are folded.

No staff have been observed. No guests. The phones in every room connect to a dial tone that does not lead anywhere documented — though one account reports reaching a recorded message in a language the wanderer did not recognize but described as "not unpleasant." The message repeated on a loop and the wanderer listened to it for what they estimated was two hours before hanging up.

ENVIRONMENT

The corridor structure is consistent: wide enough for two people side by side, ceiling height approximately 2.6 meters (8.5 ft), wall-mounted sconces providing warm but inadequate light at irregular intervals. Between sconces, the light fails almost completely. The carpet absorbs sound aggressively — footsteps are near-silent, which is both an advantage and a source of acute psychological discomfort in the dark stretches.

Room doors are numbered but the numbering follows no logical sequence. Floor indicators in elevator banks — functional in some areas, dormant in others — display numbers that do not correspond to any observable architectural floor count. Stairwells connect between corridors but not in predictable ways: descending two flights does not reliably bring a wanderer to a lower floor.

The swimming pool areas, where accessed, are filled and faintly illuminated from below by lights of unknown origin. The water is warm. Analysis is not possible but no wanderer who has consumed it has reported illness. The pool areas are considered among the safer rest locations on the level — entity presence is low there, possibly due to acoustic properties of the space.

KNOWN ENTITIES

Level 5 has one documented entity. It does not travel in groups. It does not hunt in patterns. Standard entity protocols do not apply here. What follows is a consolidated account drawn from the seventeen wanderers who have encountered it and survived long enough to report.

ENTITY FILE · SINGULAR · EXTREME ANOMALY
THE BEAST OF LEVEL 5
● HOSTILE · EXTREME · SINGULAR

Physical description: The body of a man in a well-fitted suit — dark wool, pressed, shoes polished to a mirror finish. Standing approximately 1.9 meters (6.2 ft). The hands are human in proportion but wrong at the knuckles. The head is that of a deep-sea cephalopod: a bulbous, glistening mass of mottled grey-violet tissue, tentacles hanging from the lower half in place of a jaw, two large pale eyes positioned slightly too high and too far apart, never blinking. It moves the way a man moves. It holds doors open. It rides the elevator. It stands at the end of corridors with its hands folded behind its back.

Rarity: The Beast is rarely encountered — only 17 of the 22+ documented Level 5 accounts include a sighting, and the majority of those are brief. It does not patrol or hunt actively. It is simply present, in the way a fixture is present. Wanderers report becoming aware that it has been in their peripheral vision for some time before they consciously register it.

DOCUMENTED ABILITIES
  • Hallucination induction. Wanderers in proximity begin experiencing sensory distortions — figures seen in mirrors that are not there, voices from behind closed doors, rooms that appear to have been recently occupied by people no longer present. These effects intensify the longer proximity is maintained and do not fully resolve upon departure from the level.
  • Sanity attrition. Extended exposure produces a documented and progressive cognitive deterioration — difficulty forming sentences, inability to recall the direction traveled, loss of time, a deepening conviction that the hotel is in fact the wanderer's home and that they have always lived there. This effect is the primary documented cause of death on Level 5, not direct contact.
  • Environmental manipulation. The Beast does not need to be in the same room to exert influence. Painting subjects turn to face a wanderer's position. Corridor mirrors reflect a version of the space with the lights slightly dimmer. Room numbers change between observations. Elevator doors open on floors that weren't selected. Ice machines activate as a wanderer passes. These effects cluster and intensify in proximity to the entity.
  • Passive dread emission. An aura of wrongness radiates from the entity at significant range — wanderers report awareness of its presence before visual confirmation, described as a pressure behind the eyes, a sudden absolute certainty that something nearby should not exist, and an involuntary cessation of breathing for several seconds. This sensation is distinct from standard fear and is involuntary.
  • Direct approach. In four of the seventeen accounts, the Beast moved toward the wanderer. In two of these, it stopped at conversational distance and stood without making sound or gesture for a period described as "too long." In one account it extended a hand, palm up, and waited. The wanderer ran. In one account the wanderer did not run. That account ends there.

Do not look directly at the Beast for longer than necessary to identify it. Do not acknowledge it verbally. Do not attempt to move past it in a corridor — reverse direction. If environmental manipulation symptoms begin (mirrors, paintings, room numbers), the Beast is nearby regardless of whether it is visible. Evacuate the area immediately toward the pool zones, where its influence appears diminished.

SURVIVAL GUIDELINES
  • If you experience the passive dread sensation — pressure behind the eyes, an involuntary breath-hold, a sense of wrongness — stop moving and assess your surroundings before continuing. The Beast is likely within the corridor or adjacent room.
  • If environmental effects begin (painting eyes, mirror discrepancies, changing room numbers), reverse your current route and move toward the pool areas. Do not investigate the source.
  • Use the rooms, sleep in the beds — but do not become comfortable. The sanity attrition effect is subtle in early stages. Set a departure deadline before sleeping and adhere to it.
  • The pool areas are the safest zones on the level. The Beast's influence is measurably diminished there. Prioritize pool-adjacent rooms for rest.
  • If you see the Beast at the end of a corridor, turn around. Do not wait to see if it moves. It has time. You do not.
  • Do not look at mirrors for longer than necessary to check for the Beast's reflection. Several accounts describe the mirror image as the primary vector of the hallucination effect.
