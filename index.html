DESCRIPTION

Level 0 is the most commonly reported initial entry point into the Backrooms. It manifests as an enormous, seemingly infinite expanse of empty office space — characterized by aged, mono-yellow wallpaper, stained damp carpet, and the persistent hum of fluorescent lights that stretch endlessly overhead.

No windows. No doors. No visible ceiling beyond the light fixtures. The layout follows no architectural logic: corridors fold back on themselves, rooms appear and disappear, and long-term navigation by memory is considered unreliable and potentially dangerous.

The smell has been consistently described across hundreds of accounts as wet carpet and old adhesive. The fluorescent lights flicker at irregular intervals. Some wanderers have reported brief, complete blackouts lasting seconds to minutes. What happens during these blackouts is largely undocumented.

ENVIRONMENT

Temperature is reported consistently between 18–22°C (64–72°F). Humidity is high, typically above 65%, which accounts for the dampness of surfaces despite no apparent water source. The carpet — a mottled, institutional yellow-beige — shows signs of old staining in many areas but appears structurally intact.

Sound does not carry normally. Footsteps are muffled. Voices feel absorbed by the walls. Several accounts describe hearing distant footsteps or sounds that do not belong to the wanderer — though no source has ever been confirmed on Level 0.

KNOWN ENTITIES
SMILERS
● HOSTILE · CONFIRMED PRESENCE
Detected primarily during light failures. Distinguished by a pair of luminescent eyes visible in darkness. Do not approach. Do not acknowledge. Maintain calm movement toward light.
FACELINGS
● PASSIVE · CONFIRMED PRESENCE
Humanoid figures lacking facial features. Generally non-aggressive. Avoid sustained eye contact and sudden movement. They have not been reported to initiate contact.
DULLERS
● THREAT LEVEL UNCLEAR
Rare accounts. Described as moving through the environment without sound. Behavioural patterns inconclusive. Treat as potentially hostile until further documentation.

Entity encounters on Level 0 increase significantly during light failures. If lights begin to flicker, move immediately toward the brightest accessible area. Do not remain stationary in dimly lit corridors.

SURVIVAL GUIDELINES
  • Do not run. Rapid movement draws attention and rapidly depletes caloric reserves in an environment with no confirmed food sources.
  • Move toward sound. Distant hum variations may indicate structural differences — potential exit points or transitions to other levels.
  • Mark your path if possible. The environment is disorienting by nature; physical markers help prevent circular wandering.
  • Maintain psychological stability. Panic has been documented as the primary cause of poor decision-making and fatal outcomes on Level 0.
  • Exit method: Noclipping through a structurally weakened wall section — typically identified by a subtle warping or visual distortion — is the most documented method of progression.
  • Do not separate from companions. Solo wanderers have a significantly lower documented return rate.
EXIT POINTS

The primary documented exit from Level 0 leads to Level 1 (The Warehouse). Transition occurs via noclipping through certain wall sections — identifiable by a faint visual shimmer or tactile softness in the surface material. The exact conditions that make a wall passable are not fully understood.

Some accounts mention spontaneous transitions occurring during light failures, but given the hazard conditions during such events, this method is not recommended.

DESCRIPTION

Level 1 presents a dramatic shift from Level 0. Where the Lobby is intimate and office-like, the Warehouse is vast and industrial — an endless concrete expanse of shipping crates, metal shelving, loading bays, and rusted machinery. The ceiling is lost to darkness even during periods of light. The scale is deeply disorienting.

Lighting here is inconsistent and unreliable. Functional fluorescent strips illuminate some areas; large sections remain in permanent near-darkness. The source of any light in the dark zones is unknown. Several wanderers have noted that light conditions appear to shift between visits to the same physical location — as though the level is not static.

Unlike Level 0, Level 1 contains material resources: water is reliably found in pipes throughout the level, and sealed food containers have been recovered from shelving units. This makes Level 1 a critical survival zone for wanderers who establish themselves within it — though the entity presence significantly complicates long-term habitation.

ENVIRONMENT

The floor is rough concrete, frequently cracked and uneven. Wanderers have reported deep gouges and drag marks in the flooring that pre-date any known human presence. Temperatures range from 12–18°C (54–64°F) — noticeably cooler than Level 0. The air carries a metallic, industrial smell overlaid with rust and machine oil.

Sound propagates strangely. In open areas, footsteps echo with unusual persistence. In corridors between shelving units, sound is deadened. Machinery can be heard operating somewhere in the distance — rhythmic, mechanical sounds that never seem to get closer regardless of direction traveled.

Several survivors describe discovering what appear to be makeshift campsites — bedding, rationed supplies, notes — apparently left by previous wanderers. The condition of these sites varies wildly. Some appear recently abandoned; others have been undisturbed for what may be years.

KNOWN ENTITIES
HOUNDS
● HOSTILE · HIGH FREQUENCY
Quadrupedal entities resembling dogs in silhouette, but wrong in proportion and movement. Highly aggressive. Hunt by sound. Silent movement is the primary defense — do not run.
CRAWLERS
● HOSTILE · CONFIRMED
Encountered in dark zones and under shelving units. Fast-moving on all fours. Have not been clearly observed — accounts describe the sound of movement and impact. Avoid low-light areas entirely.
FACELINGS
● PASSIVE · PRESENT
Also present on Level 1 in higher density than Level 0. Behavior remains non-aggressive. Some wanderers report them clustered near resource locations — water pipes, food shelving.
CLUMPS
● BEHAVIOR VARIABLE
Large, slow-moving masses of unclear composition. Accounts conflict on threat level. Some report them as passive; others describe aggressive behavior following prolonged proximity. Maintain distance.

Hound activity increases dramatically in the dark sectors of Level 1. Current recommendation is to avoid any area where ambient light drops below functional visibility. If caught in darkness — stop moving, control breathing, and wait.

AVAILABLE RESOURCES
PIPED WATER
● COMMON
Exposed pipe networks run throughout much of Level 1. Water from functional pipes has been tested and consumed without recorded illness. Boiling is nonetheless advised.
SEALED RATIONS
● UNCOMMON
Industrial food containers found on shelving units. Contents vary. Most are in adequate condition. Check seals carefully — compromised containers have caused illness.
ALMOND WATER
● RARE · HIGH VALUE
Distinctive plastic bottles found infrequently throughout the level. Consumption restores significant energy and appears to have mild calming properties. Source unknown.
ABANDONED GEAR
● UNCOMMON
Previous wanderers' equipment found at campsites. Quality varies. Torches, rope, clothing, and written notes have all been recovered. Notes may contain navigational value.
SURVIVAL GUIDELINES
  • Prioritize light sources immediately upon arrival. A torch or functional lighting dramatically reduces entity encounter risk.
  • Secure water before exploring further. Pipe locations near the entry zone from Level 0 are the most reliably documented.
  • Search abandoned campsites carefully. Prior occupants' notes have navigated wanderers to exits; this information is invaluable.
  • Do not move through dark sectors under any circumstances if Hound activity has been detected in the vicinity.
  • The machinery sounds serve as a rough compass. Wanderers who followed the sound consistently have reported reaching structural anomalies that led to level transitions.
  • If establishing a camp, choose an elevated position with at least one clear sightline. Ground-level positions have a worse documented survival rate.
